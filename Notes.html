What is the Chain Rule?

The chain rule is used to differentiate composite functions. If a function y depends on u, and u depends on x, then the derivative of y with respect to x is found by multiplying the derivatives together.

dy/dx = (dy/du) × (du/dx)

When to use it

Use the chain rule whenever you see a function nested inside another function — sometimes called a "function of a function." The key is identifying the outer and inner functions correctly.

  • Outer function: the main operation applied last
  • Inner function: the expression plugged into the outer function
  • Differentiate outer, then multiply by derivative of inner

Example

Find the derivative of f(x) = (3x² + 1)⁵

f'(x) = 5(3x² + 1)&sup4; × 6x = 30x(3x² + 1)&sup4;
Exam tip: Always simplify after applying the chain rule. Leaving the answer in expanded form loses marks if further factorisation is possible.

Practice problems

Try differentiating: sin(x²), e^(3x+1), and √(2x−5). Use the chain rule step by step for each and verify by expanding where possible.
